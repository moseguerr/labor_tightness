About This Study

{% if session_type == 'study1' %}

The phrases you classified came from real U.S. job postings. We are studying how employers communicate different types of information to job seekers — including statements about organizational mission and purpose, employee treatment, compensation, job duties, and qualifications.

Your classifications will help us validate a coding system that categorizes these phrases automatically across millions of job postings. This research aims to understand how employers use different types of language when competing for talent.

{% elif session_type == 'study2' %}

The job postings you evaluated were fictional, created specifically for this study. We systematically varied three aspects of the postings: (1) whether the company emphasized a social mission, (2) whether it emphasized being a great workplace, and (3) the salary offered.

We are studying how these features — particularly claims about organizational purpose and mission — influence job seekers' evaluations. This research investigates whether organizational purpose claims function as genuine signals of employer quality or as substitutes for concrete benefits like salary.

{% else %}

In Part 1, the phrases you classified came from real U.S. job postings. We are studying how employers communicate different types of information to job seekers. Your classifications will help validate our automated coding system.

In Part 2, the job postings were fictional, created specifically for this study. We systematically varied the company's framing (social mission vs. good workplace vs. neutral) and salary level. We are studying how organizational purpose claims influence job seekers' evaluations and whether these claims function as genuine signals or as substitutes for concrete benefits.
