Phrase Sorting Game

How it works

Phrases will fall from the top of the screen. Click the bucket where you think each phrase belongs before it reaches the bottom.

Sort as many as you can in 5 minutes. The phrases will speed up as the game goes on. If you miss one, it will come back around later.

The five categories:

Organizational Purpose — Some employers describe what they stand for beyond making money — what motivates them, what they are trying to build or change, and why that matters. This shapes how the organization operates and motivates internally and how it engages with the world outside. When a firm’s purpose resonates with you, the work feels meaningful because it reflects something you genuinely care about.
Good Employer — Some employers describe the work environment they create for people inside it to make the experience of working there better. This can be independent of the organization’s mission.
Compensation & Benefits — Some employers describe the return for your work — what you receive in exchange for your time and effort. The common thread is that these are forms of value the employer distributes to you as part of the employment exchange.
Job Tasks & Requirements — Some employers describe what the role actually involves — the responsibilities, skills required, and what the work looks like day to day. This language is about the job itself rather than the employer.
Not Sure — Use this bucket if a phrase could reasonably belong in more than one category, or if you cannot tell what it is communicating. There is no penalty for using it.

Tip: You can also use keyboard keys 1 through 5 to sort faster. There are no wrong answers — we want your honest instinct.
