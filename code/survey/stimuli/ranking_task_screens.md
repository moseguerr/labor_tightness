# Ranking Task Screens — Stage 1

All five ranking tasks are presented sequentially (Study 3) or three ranking tasks (Study 2: Dimensions 1, 3, and 5). On each screen, participants see all five postings as draggable cards and rank them from 1 (highest) to 5 (lowest) on the specified dimension.

Wages are hidden across all five postings during Stage 1. Each posting displays "Salary: Undisclosed."

---

## General Instruction (shown once, before Dimension 1)

> You will now complete a series of ranking tasks. For each task, drag the five job postings into order based on the specific question asked. Position 1 is the highest and position 5 is the lowest. There are no right or wrong answers — we are interested in your impressions.

---

## Dimension 1 — Perceived Pay

**Label shown to participant:** "Expected Salary"

**Instruction text:**

> Rank these employers from the one you think offers the highest salary to the one you think offers the lowest — even though salary is not shown. Use whatever cues you have from each posting.

**Helper text (smaller font, below instruction):**

> Consider anything in the posting that gives you a sense of what this employer might pay, including the type of role, the company description, and how the posting is written.

---

## Dimension 2 — Employer Investment

**Label shown to participant:** "Investment in You"

**Instruction text:**

> Rank these employers from the one you think would most invest in your development and treat you best as an employee, to the one that would invest the least.

**Helper text:**

> Think about which employer would put the most resources into your growth, well-being, and career trajectory.

---

## Dimension 3 — Values and Mission Alignment

**Label shown to participant:** "Values Alignment"

**Instruction text:**

> Rank these employers from the one whose values and mission align most closely with your own, to the one whose values align least.

**Helper text:**

> Consider which organization you feel most connected to in terms of what it stands for and what it is trying to accomplish.

---

## Dimension 4 — Learning and Professional Growth

**Label shown to participant:** "Learning and Growth"

**Instruction text:**

> Rank these employers from the one where you think you would learn the most and grow most professionally, to the one where you would grow the least.

**Helper text:**

> Consider both formal development opportunities and the nature of the work itself — where would you build the strongest skills and knowledge?

---

## Dimension 5 — Overall Desirability

**Label shown to participant:** "Overall Desirability"

**Instruction text:**

> Setting aside any single factor, rank these employers from the one you would most want to work for to the one you would least want to work for.

**Helper text:**

> Give your overall impression. Which posting makes you most want to apply?

---

## Implementation Notes

- **Study 2:** Present Dimensions 1, 3, and 5 only (Perceived Pay, Values Alignment, Overall Desirability). Skip Dimensions 2 and 4.
- **Study 3:** Present all five dimensions.
- Posting cards should display: **Company Name**, **Job Title**, **Brief excerpt** (first 1-2 sentences of the company description section), and "Salary: Undisclosed."
- Randomize the initial ordering of the five cards for each participant and each dimension to avoid position effects.
- Record the final ranking order and the timestamp for each dimension completion.
- Enforce that all five cards are placed before the participant can advance.
- If pilot testing shows sessions exceeding 28 minutes, **remove Dimension 4** (Learning and Growth) first — it is the least theoretically essential (Study 3 only; Study 2 already excludes it).
