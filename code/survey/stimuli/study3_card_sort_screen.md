# Hiring Manager Card Sort — Study 3, Stage 2

Presented after Stage 1 rankings are complete. Wage visibility is applied per arm assignment: Arm A participants see salary ranges on all postings; Arm B participants see "Salary: Undisclosed." One card sort per posting, presented in random order across participants.

## Card Sort Instruction (shown for each posting)

> For each posting below, select the **three things** you would most want to add or strengthen to make it more attractive to strong candidates.

*[Display the full posting text above the card pool. Arm A: salary visible. Arm B: "Salary: Undisclosed."]*

## Card Pool

12 cards total. Participant selects exactly 3 per posting. Cards are displayed in randomized order for each posting. All 12 cards should be visually identical in size, font, and prominence — no card type should appear more or less salient than any other.

### Purpose Cards (P) — strengthening organizational identity and mission signals

| ID | Card Text |
|----|-----------|
| P1 | Strengthen the description of our mission and what we stand for |
| P2 | Add language about the impact this role has on our broader goals |
| P3 | Describe the kind of people and values that thrive here |
| P4 | Clarify what makes our organization distinct from competitors |

### Good-Employer Cards (G) — strengthening employer investment and working condition signals

| ID | Card Text |
|----|-----------|
| G1 | Add details about career development and learning opportunities |
| G2 | Highlight flexibility, hybrid arrangements, or work-life balance |
| G3 | Mention mentorship, team support, or internal mobility |

### Wage and Compensation Cards (W) — strengthening monetary and benefits signals

| ID | Card Text |
|----|-----------|
| W1 | Add or increase the stated salary range |
| W2 | Mention performance bonuses or equity opportunities |
| W3 | Highlight the overall compensation and benefits package |

### Task and Role Cards (T) — strengthening role clarity and expectations

| ID | Card Text |
|----|-----------|
| T1 | Make the role description more specific and concrete |
| T2 | Clarify what success looks like in this role in the first year |

## Recording Instructions (for survey programmer)

- Record which 3 cards were selected per posting, the order of selection, and the timestamp for each posting.
- Compute and store the card type profile per posting: P-count, G-count, W-count, T-count (each ranges 0–3, summing to 3).
- This card type profile is the primary outcome for Stage 2.
- Card labels (P1, G1, W1, T1, etc.) are NOT shown to participants. They are for researcher reference only.
- The full posting (with or without salary per arm assignment) should remain visible while the participant makes their selections — do not require them to recall from memory.
- Enforce exactly 3 selections per posting. Show a counter: "Selected: 2 of 3."
- Randomize card display order independently for each posting.
- Present postings in a random order that differs across participants.

## Theoretical Purpose

The hiring manager card sort is the supply-side parallel to the job seeker card sort in Study 2. Job seeker cards (Study 2) reveal what signals job seekers respond to when crafting a self-presentation. Hiring manager cards (Study 3) reveal what signals firms reach for when trying to attract candidates.

The field study finds firms making purpose claims offer higher wages in tight markets. If hiring managers reach for W-type cards (wages, compensation) when reviewing purpose postings, that is consistent with costly signaling from the firm side — managers recognize that purpose language needs wage backing. If they reach for more purpose language (P-type), it suggests firms may believe doubling down on purpose is sufficient without additional compensation.

The key comparison is the W-type selection rate across posting types: do hiring managers add wage signals more often to purpose postings than to good-employer or neutral postings?
