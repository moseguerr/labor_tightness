# Framing Screen — Study 3, Hiring Manager Version

Study 3 shares the same five fictional job postings and the same four-stage skeleton as Study 2. The key difference is perspective: participants adopt the role of a hiring manager rather than a job seeker.

---

## Opening Framing (displayed before Stage 1)

> Imagine you are a hiring manager at a mid-size firm. Your team is growing and you are looking to fill an analyst-level role. Your boss has asked you to review five job postings that HR is considering and give feedback on what you would strengthen or add to each one to attract strong candidates.
>
> First, read each posting carefully.

*[Display all five postings. Wages are hidden across all postings during this screen. Enforce a minimum 60-second reading period before the "Continue" button activates.]*

---

## Stage 1 Framing Note (displayed before the ranking tasks begin)

> Before giving your feedback, rank the five postings on the following dimensions — as you imagine a strong candidate would evaluate them.

**Design rationale:** Hiring managers in Stage 1 are asked to take the job seeker perspective for the rankings. This makes Stage 1 responses directly comparable across Study 2 (actual job seekers) and Study 3 (hiring managers adopting the seeker perspective). The hiring manager perspective activates in Stage 2.

---

## Transition Screen: Stage 1 → Stage 2

> Now, as the hiring manager: what would you strengthen in each posting to attract the best candidates?

## Implementation Notes

- The opening framing screen should display all five postings in their Stage 1 (wages hidden) versions.
- Posting display order should be randomized per participant and maintained through all stages.
- The Stage 1 framing note appears on a separate screen after the opening framing, immediately before the first ranking dimension.
- Transition screens are full-screen interstitial pages with a "Continue" button.
